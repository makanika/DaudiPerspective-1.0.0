This document outlines Raxio’s Peering and Interconnection Strategy, a plan to position our data centres as the continent's central gravity points for network interconnection.

Our Strategic Pillars

Our strategy is anchored in a "Zero Import" vision and built on four pillars.

1. Localize Traffic

Drastically reduce data transit outside Africa by enabling domestic and regional traffic exchange.

2. Promote Peering

Actively build and support IXP ecosystems through infrastructure, marketing, and operational support.

3. Enable Visibility

Build data-driven dashboards to report peering activity, CDN readiness, and IXP performance.

4. Support Ecosystem

Foster collaboration among ISPs, CDNs, and enterprises to strengthen interconnection across the region.

Market Execution Plan

Our proof-of-concept will focus on four key markets with tailored strategies.

Uganda: Establishing the Hub

Key Challenge:

Low awareness of IXPs; unclear peer mapping across UIXP's two sites; Raxio not yet seen as the primary peering hub.

Strategic Action Plan:

  • **Map the Ecosystem:** Analyze all networks at both UIXP sites to identify peering gaps.
  • **Drive Awareness:** Launch an internal Raxio Peering Certification and host ISP-focused workshops.
  • **Advocate:** Champion a "comply or explain" regulatory approach to encourage data localization.

Mozambique: Enabling Transition

Key Challenge:

MOZIX has low awareness and is a single-site, university-hosted platform, limiting its scalability and neutrality.

Strategic Action Plan:

  • **Promote & Support:** Launch targeted awareness campaigns and facilitate hardware imports for MOZIX.
  • **Enable Transition:** Strategically partner with MOZIX to help it evolve into a neutral, multi-site IXP.
  • **Secure Fibre:** Collaborate with partners like Movitel to explore dark fibre sponsorship to expand MOZIX's footprint.

Ethiopia: Activating a New Market

Key Challenge:

The ADDIX node in ET1 is delayed; the market is nascent with dominant telcos and low awareness of peering benefits.

Strategic Action Plan:

  • **Accelerate Go-Live:** Prioritize working with ADDIX to bring the ET1 node online.
  • **Build Market Anticipation:** Begin educational campaigns immediately, before the node is live.
  • **Foster Strategic Peering:** Position Raxio as the neutral ground for major telcos to peer.

DRC: Building a New Corridor

Key Challenge:

The strategy is being finalized to leverage new subsea cable landings and create a new African traffic corridor.

Strategic Action Plan:

  • **Finalize Strategy:** Complete the detailed plan for the DRC market.
  • **Build North-South Corridor:** Focus on interconnecting Angola, DRC, and South Africa.
  • **Bridge Regions:** Position Raxio as the key interconnection provider for the region.
